Tell Us About Them

Take your time. There are no wrong answers.

What was their full name, and what did most people call them?
e.g. "Robert James Henderson — but everyone called him Bobby"
Describe them in exactly three words.
Don't overthink it — your first instinct is usually the truest.
What made them laugh — really, genuinely laugh?
A story, a type of humor, something only your family would understand.
If they could say one last thing to everyone in that room today, what would it be?
You know them. What would they want people to walk away feeling?
What is one story — funny, touching, or both — that perfectly captures who they were?
This becomes the heart of the eulogy. The more specific, the better.
What did they love most in life — their true passions?
People, hobbies, rituals, obsessions — what lit them up?
What is the most important thing they taught you, or that you learned from watching them live?
Who are the most important people or relationships in their life?
Spouse, children, best friends, their dog — whoever made up their world.
Is there anything about them that most people didn't know — a hidden talent, a surprising side, a secret joy?
How would you like the eulogy to feel when it ends?

Your eulogy will be approximately 500 words, written to be spoken aloud in 3–4 minutes. It will include two moments of lightness, one emotional peak, and a hopeful close.
